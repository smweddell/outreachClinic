IU School of Medicine Psychiatry

The Mental Health Clinic at the Student Outreach Clinic was established in 2021 with the goal of providing a free, safe space for our neighbors in order to address any mental health concerns they may have or any concerns that the clinic has on their behalf. The goal of the Mental Health Clinic is to triage at-risk patients to both acutely treat their illnesses, as well as connect them with long-term outside mental health resources to ensure that they receive the help that they not only need, but also deserve. Neighbors can be referred to us for psychiatric screenings, diagnostic interviews, therapy recommendations and referrals, medication management, and more. Looking long-term, our executive board is working with IU Psychology PhD students to provide individual and group therapy services at the clinic.

Info about how to get involved as a student

Volunteer sign-ups are announced through the IUSM Psychiatry Student Interest Group listserve. Positions are available for MS1-MS4. We hope to expand and offer volunteering to psychology doctorate students soon. To add yourself to the mailing list, send an email message to "PSYCHSIG-L-subscribe@iupui.edu".
