About Us

The Indiana University Student Outreach Clinic is a free, student-run clinic that provides primary care-based medical, dental, social, legal services as well as other services for the uninsured and underserved in the Indianapolis community. Led by IU School of Medicine, the clinic strives to close the health care gap in the community by coordinating a medical presence to address a wide variety of conditions including infections, musculoskeletal complaints, high blood pressure, depression, etc..

Clinic History

In February of 2009, Jim Streitlemier, pastor of the nondenominational Neighborhood Fellowship Church, located in the area of 10th and Rural in Indianapolis, and Dr. Javier Sevilla, primary care physician and instructor at Methodist/IU Hospital, joined forces to utilize the church to open a free clinic to serve the poor of Indianapolis.

Since then, the clinic has continued to grow with the assistance of many community partners, including IU, UIndy and Butler University. The clinic has also been blessed to have had many hundreds of volunteers helping to organize things each week.

Founders Dr. Javier Sevilla and Pastor Jim Streitlemier

Clinic Impact

Over 1600 patients were seen at the clinic in 2019

In 2019 over 2,700 prescriptions were dispensed and medication classes were offered to ensure understanding and compliance with the prescription

The student outreach clinic has been a national leader and played an active role in the growth of student run clinics around the country.

Research

Research at the SOC is centered on service appraisal, impact measurement, and quality improvement. Through an interdisciplinary approach, the goal of the Research Team is to offer evidence-based recommendations and ensure that services at the SOC align with the standard of care. Currently, Medicine (including subgroups like Women's Health, Ophthalmology, and Mental Health Services) and Nursing are part of the current Research infrastructure, though ideally, the Research Team will grow to include members from ALL clinic partners.


In order to propose a topic, we ask that individuals fill out this form and send an email to ndevanat@iu.edu that also confirms submission. The reason contact information and name is intentionally not included on this form is to blind Research Team members when deciding whether or not to proceed with a project, to prevent favoring individuals. Once external ideas are approved, the Research Team will submit appropriate paperwork to the Indiana University Institutional Review Board to ensure the proposal adheres to strict ethical standards.
